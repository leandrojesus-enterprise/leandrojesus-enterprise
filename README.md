## 📖 My Journey (Long Version)

I’ve been passionate about computers for as long as I can remember. Growing up, I was always the “IT friend” — setting things up, fixing problems, and even running my own game servers for a small group of friends.

I started learning programming in school (10th to 12th grade), and after finishing, I chose not to go to university. Instead, I joined a 3-month Java bootcamp, where I got my first real exposure to the enterprise world — it was challenging, but very rewarding.

During that time, I began experiencing memory loss, lack of focus, and distorted thoughts about reality. I was later diagnosed with schizophrenia, a hereditary condition from my mother’s side. At that point, I stopped programming, and it felt like all my progress was lost.

Over time, things improved. I’m now in a much better place — more aware, more grounded, and able to understand when my thoughts don’t align with reality. My memory and focus aren’t as sharp as before, but I’ve learned to adapt.

Most importantly, I found the courage to return to programming.

Relearning has been incredibly meaningful — it helps me stay focused, grounded, and out of the “lala land.” It gives me structure, purpose, and a way to keep moving forward.
